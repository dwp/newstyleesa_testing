Based on your answers, you may not be eligible for New Style ESA payments

If you’ve not made enough National Insurance contributions over the last 2 to 3 years, you may not be eligible to get a New Style ESA payment. But you may be able to get National Insurance credits (opens in a new window).

New Style ESA gives you Class 1 credits, which can help to fill gaps in your National Insurance record, to make sure you qualify for certain benefits including the State Pension. If you want to get National Insurance credits only, you can continue to apply for New Style ESA.

If you’ve been on a low income or out of work, you can check if you’re eligible for Universal Credit instead. If you get Universal Credit, you will get Class 3 credits. They count towards your State Pension only.
